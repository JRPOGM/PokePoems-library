# The Dangers of Youth

<blockquote>
One day, walking along the riverbend trail<br>
I come across a baby false water cobra<br>
Tiny and young, slithering to and fro through the world<br>
Curious of the giant over you, you stare back<br>
And I imagine your future

I know your kind<br>
I know your nature<br>
I know the danger you can possess<br>
Your people’s life is such a routine<br>
So much so I know where those fangs would sink<br>
I know how tightly you’d bind<br>
I know how large your appetite could be<br>
It’s life, you’d defend<br>
The weak and the strong<br>
As prey and predator<br>
The natural selection of the world<br>
So too, would it be<br>
For me to kneel down<br>
And pinch your neck now, as tight as I can<br>
Knowing the nests it would save

But I don’t, and I won’t<br>
For the young live to learn<br>
With the right teacher, you would know<br>
The difference between necessity and gorging<br>
And I imagine your future could be better
</blockquote>

## Ekans, the Snake Pokémon

![hand-drawn artwork](/images/ekans.jpeg)

> “_Newborn Ekans do not have the toxin in their fangs that their species is known for, for many months after they hatch. Without this protective poison to ward off enemies and predators, the youngest spend their early months surrounded by the more mature of their species, coddled by their parents and protected by those of their kind with experience in surviving the wilderness. Even without the poison, an Ekans’ entire mouth is still mighty dangerous. They have the ability to unhinge their jaws and stretch the edges of their mouths to fit larger prey down their gullet, swallowing them to dissolve in their digestive track as they continue to slither about, and for creatures twice their diameter; often, though, this is simply to consume the eggs of bird Pokémon more than the bird Pokémon themselves._”

> “_For a species of anywhere between six to eight feet in length, Ekans are also surprisingly agile and quiet. Due to their size, they mostly populate in regions with waist-high grassy fields to sneak unobserved by their prey and nearby predators. With their tongues, Ekans can track the scents of nearby creatures, allowing them to redirect themselves to or from a target without having to lay eyes on them either. The most frequent sightings of Ekans are around trees, as they will wrap themselves around the trunks while they sleep, or around branches as they wait nearby the nests of bird Pokémon to learn their comings and goings._”
