# Vengeful Spirits

<blockquote>
One might think<br>
With bodies dropping, stars falling out of the skies<br>
The world would go quiet<br>
Nights would be silent<br>
Only the epicenter left in control of what remains

No<br>
Fallen stars never stop burning<br>
They may burst, but their light shines on<br>
Newborns will carry on their torch<br>
Shining on worlds and life all around<br>
They will not go silent<br>
Another Big Bang will never come to stop them<br>
The universe will live on<br>
Galaxies will spin<br>
And all will go on

No god-players will end it short<br>
No dark-matter matters more<br>
Than life
</blockquote>

## Gastly, the Gas Pokémon

![hand-drawn artwork](/images/gastly.jpeg)

> “_Said to be born from grieving souls lingering in graveyards that gather and possess the foggy air around them into a body, Gastly are actually more gaseous than they are a spirit. The gas of their bodies is the only thing that gives them form, and as far as we know makes up most of their body’s mass. This does make the Gastly’s natural enemy a strong breeze, as high-speed winds can blow their bodies apart and, if they do not find shelter fast enough, will lose their form and die again. Groups of these Pokémon can be found more easily during these storms, hiding out in the same, sturdy buildings._”

> “_Otherwise, the gas that makes their body is also their strongest weapon. Because their bodies have no solid form, they can spread their bodies to encompass a wider area without feeling torn apart, enveloping their opponents and exposing them to a poisonous substance within their gas. This poison is so powerful that even Copperajah, a Steel-type normally immune to Poison-type attacks, can be subdued within just a few seconds. If Gastly so wish, they can control the dosage of this exposure to seep through the skin of their prey to kill them before feeding._”
