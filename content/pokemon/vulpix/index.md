# Out like a Light

<blockquote>
I see them<br>
Tears in the flame<br>
Wallows in the crackling<br>
Mourns in the burning, scorched earth as it spreads<br>
It freezes my skin as it licks my eyes<br>
Boils my blood as it burns my throat

The fox, with a torch in mouth, continues to run<br>
Sets fire to the brush, lights the tree ablaze<br>
Smokes out the rabbits with the worms, birds with the bees<br>
I, tempted to chase, clench<br>
For maybe I could snuff out the source<br>
Only to be caught up in the flames with it
</blockquote>

## Vulpix, the Fox Pokemon

![hand-drawn artwork](/images/vulpix.jpeg)

> “_As a wild Pokémon, Vulpix were once a very intimidating species. Many Fire-type Pokémon have reason for other Pokémon and humans to be wary in their presence, but Vulpix specifically have an internal organ that constantly burns and generates fire that the Pokémon has to spew out to keep their bodies from overheating. The shape of these flames were, for a time, mistaken to be burning vengeful souls the Vulpix could somehow harness, and only in recent history has the rumor finally been laid to rest. Now, the Fox Pokémon has become incredibly popular among people and Pokémon Trainers. The pelt of a Vulpix is one of the most beautiful in the world, but not to be used in fashion but often as a marketing scheme for Pokémon grooming. The fur of the Vulpix species is highly demanding, constantly needing care to remain luscious and smooth and keep it untangled, so many grooming brands seek to use a Vulpix as their mascot and forefront of marketing, as a sign of their business’s dedication and hard work to maintain a Pokémon’s beauty._”

> “_The Vulpix found in Alola traded their fire for ice. Once known as the Keokeo to the native people of Alola, these Vulpix had fled the same volcanic eruption the Sandshrew did, and were exported to Ula’Ula Island. There, their bodies evolved over time to handle the cooler temperatures and eventually harness the Ice type. Their breath now falls below negative fifty-eight degrees; their tails can shoot Ice Shard with a swing; and at a microscopic scale, tiny snowflakes grow on the hairs of their fur that keep their body temperature low. They are quite popular to own in Alola to combat higher temperatures, using the cold air they emit in confined spaces to cool down houses and businesses during bad heat waves through the region._”
