Dancing in the Moonlight

I believe she is watching, way up high
That where her light shines when nothing brighter is seen

I slide around on dew-kissed hills
Swing in and out between silver-shaded trees
My skin sparkling in her light, no brighter than the stars above her
No attempt to outshine her beauty

I’m out there for her
To remind her she is not alone
That me and many more remain by her side and her sight
Because I am sure she is watching
Afraid, I think, to blink
As if it will erase us all

So I greet her in the mornings when she shows
Perform in the nights when she stares
Believing she is watching me, as I remember her
So when I am gone, she would remember, too
The microorganisms that danced in her eyes
