The Job Market of Wild West

Disillusionment is not unwarranted
When shots are fired and you are where the smoke points
Despite your gun still holstered on your hip

You are not the sheriff or the law
But you are armed, as you are wise to be
Prepared to one day stare down the barrel too

Only now, the townsfolk fear their sheriff
Man and woman expecting to be shot in the back
The moment they dare to step off the office porch

It is no wonder tumbleweeds are outgrowing the population
The weed’s responsibilities never change
Nor does the dirt nor the wind threaten their very being

It is expected to feel estranged
When the railway comes into town
And the train makes a mad dash with the money
