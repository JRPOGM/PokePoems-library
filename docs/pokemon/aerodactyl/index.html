Jaws of Life

The morbid humor of it all
As fire fighter and policemen haul out the great white of machinery
To sink its teeth into the poor woman’s car
The poster boy of cutting your losses

Aerodactyl, the Fossil Pokémon

“Believed to be the ‘king of the skies’ back in the time they were alive, Aerodactyl is a powerful ancient species of Pokémon. Like Omanyte and Kabuto, they were first revived into the modern day through genetic code preserved in amber, and like the other two they broke out from captivity shortly after their revival to retake mountainous regions as their king species. Aerodactyl are far more vicious than the other fossil Pokémon though, keen to attack first and tear through prey with their saw-like teeth, so restoration of this species has been permanently banned by the governing bodies within each region with the technology to revive fossilized Pokémon. Combatting the return of Aerodactyl to the wild is also being handled by approved League officials in the regions where they have been released or escaped.”
“These are also not the true form of Aerodactyl revived to perfect accuracy. Thanks to Mega Evolution in the hands of trusted trainers, we can witness a form of Aerodactyl whose dormant genetic code is awakened and covers their external skin in a multitude of sharp rocks. Scholars today believe this form is the true state of Aerodactyl, one we could not restore correctly with only the fraction of DNA preserved in amber; others detest this, as the power of Mega Evolution puts the Aerodactyl in a state of harm simply being transformed, and it is likely this is actually an overcorrection empowering the genetic material of animated amber and rock that bled into the fossil’s restoration process. Regardless, it has sparked the hypothesis for scientists that every fossil Pokémon may be an imperfect recreation of their original species, as the materials of their preservation could have made up space in the empty pockets of the retained DNA.”
